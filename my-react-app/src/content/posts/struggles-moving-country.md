Reflections on the struggles of moving to another country and tips to take care of your mental and emotional health.

---

Moving to another country is not as rosy as the media portrays.

Yes, Canada is a peaceful place to raise a family.  
Yes, Canada has one of the best education systems for kids in the world.

However, it is not just about packing your suitcase and boarding the airplane.

There are so many emotional rollercoasters that you will ride on.

And it can feel very overwhelming.

---

Have you left everything you had in your home country behind, and come here hoping for a better future for your children — only to realize that Canada is not as wonderful as you anticipated?

Have you ever thought to yourself:

> _Had I known this could happen, I would have never come here._

But now… you can’t go back.

---

Are you having an identity crisis?

Are you someone who has sacrificed everything for your family — and lost yourself in the process?

You studied so hard back home to become a doctor.

And yet, when you moved here, you had to start all over again.

Your credentials don’t count anymore.

You can’t go back to school because you need to make money right away to support your immediate family.

And in many cases, you also need to send money back home to support your elderly parents.

After searching for so long, you finally land a job.

But it pays so little.  
And demands so much physical labor.

You work long hours.  
You are exhausted by the time you get home.

Your husband works all day too.

However, when he gets home, he sits down and watches TV.

There are meals to cook.  
Dishes to clean.  
Kids to take care of.

Once again, you toughen yourself up and do what needs to get done.

And when your husband casually asks:

> “How was your day?”

You explode.

You might feel guilty about your reaction.

But you can’t help it.

Can’t he see how your day is going?  
Why can’t he help with the housework?  
It must be nice just to sit there and do nothing.

---

On top of that, it hurts the most when your efforts seem to have become in vain.

When one of your children is struggling with bullying at school.

When your husband feels stuck in his low-paid-but-high-demand job.

When you know that you are meant for more — but your hands are tied with so many obligations.

---

I have seen many situations like this.

And here are some practical tips that I find helpful:

---

## 1. Remember: This is only one chapter

What you are going through right now is only one chapter in your life.

It may feel hard and hopeless.

But there can be better days ahead.

Never give up hope.

Never let anyone take that away from you.

---

## 2. Take care of yourself

Think of the safety instructions on an airplane:

> You need to put on your own oxygen mask first before helping others.

Make time for self-care.

Even five minutes a day matters.

- Go for a short workout
- Read a few pages of a book
- Do arts and crafts
- Listen to music
- Do anything that lights you up

You deserve that time.

---

## 3. Connect with others

Call someone you can relate to — whether they are here or back home.

Schedule a coffee date with another mom when your kids are at school and you have a day off.

You are not meant to carry everything alone.

---

## 4. Ask for help

Know that you do not have to struggle alone.

You are not the only one going through this.

Pushing your anxieties and worries aside will only make them grow bigger.

Face them head-on — but with proven strategies and support.

---

## 5. Remember who you are

You are not what happens to you.

You are how you respond to what happens.

It is not too late to fix what went wrong.

As long as you keep trying, you can make the best out of what life brings your way.

As the old saying goes:

> When life gives you lemons, make lemonade.
